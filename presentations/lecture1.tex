	$y'''+4y''+3y'=x^2\cos x-3x$
	First, we solve for $y_c$. The auxiliary equation is 
	\begin{eqnarray*}
		m^3+4m^2+3m&=m(m^2+4m+3)
		&=m(m+3)(m+1)\\
		m&=0, -3, -1
	\end{eqnarray*}
	Hence $y_c=c_1+c_2e^{-3}x+c_3e^{-1}$.\par
	For the left side, the annihilator will be 
	$$(D^3+4D^2+3D)y$$ $$=D(D^2+4D+3)y$$
	$$=D(D+3)(D+1)y$$
